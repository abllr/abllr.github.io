More Talk

Bitcoin uses 10 minute blocks , a study made by some more smart fellows paper once a miner (in Bitcoin) mines a block it takes 6.5 seconds to reach half the nodes , and 40 seconds to reach the majority (95.6 seconds) the mean delay is 12.6 seconds (average time for a mined block to reach other nodes) . Lower blocktime introduces stale blocks , where a miner Y will still be mining block B1 while a miner Z has mined block B2 on top of B1 , simply Y didn’t yet receive B1 to understand it has to move forward .

Lower blocktime brings faster transactions as each transaction has to be included in a block, having a blocktime of 10 seconds means it will take 10+-2 seconds for a transaction to be included in a mined block , this is a neat trick to use if the second fallacy didn’t exist and everyone had similar internet speed and lived on one single point on EART . If two miners with similar hash power exist in a network ,the winner will always be the closest to the majority of nodes in simpler terms if miner A is in France , miner B in Australia and the majority is in Europe A has more chances to win than B thus your network is more centralized than first thought . A Network is as strong as the it’s hashpower and how distributed it is .
