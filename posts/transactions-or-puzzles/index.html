Transactions as Puzzles : How to think about Bitcoin and Ethereum ledgers

Bitcoin is digital P2P cash , the important part here is cash, unlike other models of transfer of value Bitcoin transactions are difficult to think about and model (do you actually know how it works ?) . ethereum ledger is similar to a what a bank database may look like . It has a set of accounts and the ledger stores states at each block (bitcoin stores the state transition), each new block represents essentially the result of a state transformation . In this blog post I’d like to explain in details the difference between the two since,because many i’ve seen don’t grasp the difference and the mental model used to represent what a real life transaction is like .

P.S : This was written mainly to give developers and normal people a mental model on how to tinker with transactions,if you are a Blockchain Expert you don’t need this .

What is money ?

I can’t answer in question , I doubt anyone can give a precise definition of money because it’s more of a representation than an actual object .

In The Bitcoin Standard book money is defined as :

A good that assumes the role of a widely accepted medium of exchange is called money , Carl Menger’s Salability adds to the definition the ease which a good can be sold on the market whenever its holder desires with the least loss in its price or value .

Let’s think about a real life transaction , you wake up in the morning and find yourself without coffee being a coffee maniac you go to the closest store to buy some of those fancy 15$ Arabica Grains that were cultivated in Puerto Rico . At the counter , you say hi to the cashier with a grimmy face you want him to know that you didn’t have that morning coffee but you’re just an A actually,you take out your wallet you have two choices :

  • Pay him with a 20$ Bill
  • Pay him with your Credit Card

The first choice is similar to Bitcoin , the second similar to Ethereum .

  • If you give him a 20$ Bill what happens is that 15$ goes to the cashier’s wallet , and 5$ goes back to your wallet this is a state transition
  • If you give him your credit card he will swipe , and your bank account balance will now be 15$ less this is a state

Bitcoin Transactions

Bitcoin Transactions are like paying someone with cash bills , and since they do not have a central mint , the blockchain stores those transactions . The transaction to be accepted in a decentralized system (that is without a central issuer) they need to provide certain proofs :

  • Where did the bill come from
  • Where is it going

The first is what we define in Bitcoin as input the second is what we define as output , but since we can’t just refeer to people by names in the blockchain, there’s what we call a SCRIPT that regulates this transition , in other words imaging instead of giving the cashier the money you put the money in a crowded street and tell people ONLY ALLOW THE PERSON WHO GIVES THIS SECRET to take the money , the secret here is the hash of the public key .

Bitcoin transactions are similar to cash in their split behavior and similar to puzzles in their spending conditions .

Ethereum Transactions

Ethereum blockchain as we said earlier stores states that is a series of accounts (0x….) and balances and a nonce . The nonce is a counter of the transactions made by the account this is a double-spending protection measure .

Each transaction , is similar to a credit card swipe , it checks whether you have the required balance and then “transfers the funds” and substract them from your initial balance .

Smarty Pants Thinking

Let’s try to give a smart explanation of what we just said :

  • Ethereum stores a series of states : S,S’,S” …
  • Bitcoin stores a series of state transitions : T,T’,T” …

This was purposefuly small to just give you an idea about how to think about both blockchains .
